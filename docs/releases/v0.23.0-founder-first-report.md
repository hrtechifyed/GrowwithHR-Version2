# v0.23.0 — Founder-first assessment and report

## Goal

Make the GrowWithHR assessment ask every governed input used by the report and make the final light and dark PDFs understandable to a first-time founder without removing the detailed legal reference.

## Assessment changes

Story 3 now includes a universal **Workforce and statutory eligibility** section for every industry. It asks:

- which workforce categories are used;
- whether women are employed;
- whether any employee is likely to fall within the current ESI wage-eligibility limit; and
- whether any employee is likely to fall within the statutory bonus eligibility limit.

No individual salary is requested. `Not sure` remains available when payroll or operations must confirm the answer. Manufacturing, BPO/ITES and Software/SaaS questions continue as separate industry-specific follow-ups.

## Founder-first report story

The report now guides the reader through:

1. **Present** — organisation snapshot, founder five-minute summary, law-status meaning and missing information.
2. **Understand** — positive foundations and a plain-language compliance operating rhythm.
3. **Act** — strategic recommendations, cross-referenced compliance actions and a 0–90-day roadmap.
4. **Future** — upcoming workforce and operating triggers.
5. **Reference** — detailed law cards, official sources, governed-law table and important information.

## Law presentation

- Every status count includes a plain-language explanation and the laws in that category.
- The threshold reason appears immediately below the Act name.
- Singular and plural workforce wording is generated correctly.
- Every actionable law receives an `A1`, `A2`, etc. identifier used again in the roadmap and annexure.
- Required-input coverage uses a ten-segment visual bar plus numeric text.
- Missing segments are accompanied by the exact question the user must answer.
- The Governed Law Index is rendered as a four-column table: law/status, general trigger, current state and next step.
- Official-law links remain visible, underlined and clickable.

## Content simplification

The separate `Selected by you` and `Company DNA suggestion` blocks are removed from the final report. Strategic priorities are presented as one leadership agenda. The old source-labelled executive-summary statements and leadership-attention pages are removed and replaced by a founder five-minute brief.

## Governance rhythm

The Compliance Review explains five operating rhythms:

- assign an owner;
- retain evidence;
- review on a defined cadence;
- escalate exceptions; and
- reassess after material change.

## Presentation consistency

Both light and true-black dark variants use the same section order, action IDs, status meanings, law table and roadmap. The final page restores the HRTechify logo and includes a reminder to refresh the advisory after a material workforce or operating change.

## Safety boundary

Input coverage remains separate from legal certainty. The report does not create an overall compliance score and continues to state that legal conclusions should be confirmed using current official sources and qualified advice.
